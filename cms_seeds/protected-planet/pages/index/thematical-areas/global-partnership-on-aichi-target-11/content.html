Aichi Target 11 on protected and conserved areas is one of the 20 targets of the Strategic Plan for Biodiversity 2011-2020. This plan provides an overarching framework for the conservation of biodiversity and guides implementation by state parties. Target 11 aspires 'By 2020, at least 17 per cent of terrestrial and inland water areas and 10 per cent of coastal and marine areas, especially areas of particular importance for biodiversity and ecosystem services, are conserved through effectively and equitably managed, ecologically representative and well-connected systems of protected areas and other effective area-based conservation measures, and integrated into the wider landscape and seascape'.

Less than two years away from the end of the Strategic Plan, further concerted efforts are required to reach some elements of the target by 2020.

What is the Partnership?

In recognition of the important work that remains to be done by 2020, the Global Partnership on Aichi Target 11 was formed to facilitate the achievement of the target. It provides a platform for all parties and partners to express their intent and reiterate their commitments towards achieving the target. The Partnership also seeks to align global, regional and national implementation of priority actions in a coordinated manner among all relevant stakeholders and rightsholders.

The partnership is composed of a large number of coordination agencies and sub-regional implementation networks, each operating with different roles and responsibilities, to ensure a coordinated and effective implementation of actions to reach the target by 2020.

How will the commitments be tracked?

Commitments made by parties, partners and individuals are part of a concerted effort to achieve the target in recognition of the importance of protected areas as a tool for conserving biodiversity and to acknowledge efforts made by the protected area community to make these areas work.

The pledges and commitments made on the platform will help to inform and complement existing tracking mechanisms on the progress achieved towards reaching Aichi Target 11.

  • Status: the Protected Planet Report series help track international progress towards achieving the different Aichi Targets, especially Aichi Target 11. The latest report was published in November 2018. The online version of the report can be accessed here.
  • Commitments: The monthly releases of the WDPA, with the inclusion of newly designated protected areas and/or expansion of existing protected areas, help to track the implementation of these commitments. In addition, commitments marine commitments can be tracked here. Commitments made here link to commitments made to the Action Agenda for Nature and the People.
  • Opportunities and gaps: Opportunities and gaps will be tracked through quarterly reports analysing the implementation of the different commitments and progress towards the different elements of the Target.
