The total number of protected area records in the July 2017 release of the WDPA is 234,008 comprising of 215,427 polygons and 18,581 point, covering 245 countries and territories.

Follow us on facebook @ Protected Planet and twitter: @protectedplanet

Highlights:

Sweden: Update of two Natura 2000 sites in Northern Sweden, Torne och Kalix älvsystem and Piteälven, both sites are river networks.

United Kingdom:Partial update of protected areas, including the addition of 117 privately protected areas in Scotland. These protected areas have been added as the result of an International Union for the Conservation of Nature (IUCN) project, Putting Nature on the Map, which aims to capture the full range of protected areas in the UK

Antarctica/ Areas Beyond National Jurisdiction: Partial update, resulting in the inclusion of five proposed records composing the Ross Sea Region Marine Protected Area. The site has been proposed by the Commission for the Convention on Conservation of Antarctic Marine Living Resources (CCAMLR) and will become a designated protected area of around 2 million km2 in December 2017.
