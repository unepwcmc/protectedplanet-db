The total number of protected area records in the June 2019 release of the World Database on Protected Areas (WDPA) is 242,843 comprising 222, 075 polygons and 20,768 points, covering 245 countries and territories.

Follow us on Facebook @ Protected Planet and Twitter: @protectedplanet

Highlights:

Peru: We receive updates from Peru every few months with changes. This update includes the addition of 5 new terrestrial sites: Naturaleza Viva Ryo (0.2km2), Masheke (3.6km2), Páramos y Bosques Montanos, Paraíso de la Comunidad Campesina San Felipe (19.5km2), Knoya - Supru (25.5km2), Bosques de Shunté y Mishollo (1914km2).and the removal of one site: Huamanmarca - Ochuro - Tumpullo.

It also involves an attribute update to ownership type, governance type, status, IUCN management category, management authority and management plan links to 97 sites in line with the WDPA schema. The attributes of the other 133 sites (Areas de Conservación Privada) will be updated next month.

Chile: This represents a small partial update of CHL's protected area network, adding two new sites both designated in 2019 (Alacalufes Wildlife Reserve Reservation and Kawésqar National Park), which combined cover ~50,000 km2 in along the southwest coastline of Patagonia. This is the second of a series of updates from Chile who provide sites as and when they are designated to UNEP-WCMC.

Kenya: This was an update to the attributes of one site: Mara North Community Conservancy. The IUCN management category was changed to VI, and the Governance Type to 'Shared governance'.

Palestine, State of: No change to the data - it was verified as correct

South Africa: A complete annual update for all protected areas of South Africa. An addition of 54 terrestrial sites to the database, 35 of which were designated in 2018. Of the new sites there are a mixture of nature reserves, protected environments, and two forest nature reserves.
