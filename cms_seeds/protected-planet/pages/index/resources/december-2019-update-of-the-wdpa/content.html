The total number of protected area records in the December 2019 release of the World Database on Protected Areas (WDPA) is 245,900 comprising 225,247 polygons and 20,653 points, covering 245 countries and territories.

December 2019 also sees the first release of the World Database on other effective area-based conservation measures (WD-OECM). The WD-OECM can be combined with the WDPA to reveal how measures beyond protected areas contribute to conservation networks. It can be downloaded here.

Follow us on Facebook @ Protected Planet and Twitter: @protectedplanet

Highlights:

Guyana: a complete update of national designations.

Zambia: the first update of sites under the Department of National Parks and Wildlife (DNPW), formerly Zambian Wildlife Authority, since 2003. Updates include the addition of 9 sites; Game Management Areas (which surround National Parks), Wildlife Sanctuaries and a National Park (Lusaka). Zambia now has 36 Game Management Areas, and 59 parks under the authority of DNPW.

Saint Helena, Ascension and Tristan da Cunha: addition of a very large marine protected area, designated as part of the UK's Blue Belt Programme in August 2019.

Papua New Guinea: the first full dataset for PNG verified by the national government. Data for sites not updated in 11 - 14 years have now been reviewed and edited. This update was facilitated by The Nature Conservancy and the Secretariat for the Pacific Regional Environment Programme, supported by the BIOPAMA programme.

Armenia: addition of the Caucasus Wildlife Refuge, a privately protected area established by the Foundation for the Protection of Wildlife and Cultural Assets.

New Caledonia: update of protected areas in New Caledonia's southern province.
