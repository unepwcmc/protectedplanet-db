[wysiwyg content] The total number of protected area records in the August 2020 release of the World Database on Protected Areas (WDPA) is 261,766 comprising 240,689 polygons and 21,077 points, covering 245 countries and territories. August 2020 also sees another release of the World Database on other effective area-based conservation measures (WD-OECM). The WD-OECM can be combined with the WDPA to reveal how measures beyond protected areas contribute to conservation networks. It can be downloaded here. Follow us on Facebook @Protected Planet and Twitter: @protectedplanet Highlights: Europe: This represents the only Nationally designated areas (CDDA) update for 2020 from the European Environment Agency (EEA). As per previous CDDA updates it constitutes an update to over 100,000 nationally designated sites across Europe. Of particular note are the changes to Switzerland (3591 new sites), Estonia (1122 new sites), Finland (999 new sites) and Sweden (256 new sites). Guinea-Bissau: This is a complete update for Guinea-Bissau which includes the addition of the Cuntabane Faunal Migratory Corridor. For 19 of the sites this is the first time they been verified since 1988. United Republic of Tanzania: A partial update for Tanzania including the addition of missing status years for 378 sites. South Africa: Complete update for South Africa, including the addition of 6 new sites: Lapalala Nature Reserve, Lekgalametsi Nature Reserve, Areb Nature Reserve, Marietjie van Niekerk Nature Reserve, Karas Nature Reserve and Meerkat National Park.
