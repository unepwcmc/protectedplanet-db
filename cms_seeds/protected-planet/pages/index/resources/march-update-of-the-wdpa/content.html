The total number of protected area records in the March 2016 release of the WDPA is 220,680 comprising of 201,728 polygons and 18,952 points.

Highlights:

Australia: Complete update of all national-level records. The WDPA includes a total of 10,897 protected area records for Australia. This dataset no longer contains point data and provides boundary information for each record..

European Environment Agency: (EEA) Common Database on Designated Areas (CDDA) – Complete update of 49 countries and territories with 4,114 polygons added and the total area covered increasing by ~134,000 km². Major changes include: the addition of 43,473 km² terrestrial protected area for Poland, 3,200 km² for France, 1,100 km² for Germany and 781 km² for Iceland. In the marine environment, the UK has added an additional 60,390 km² and Spain an additional 4,253 km².

Timor-Leste: Complete update, including the removal of one polygon and changes to a further 25 protected areas.
