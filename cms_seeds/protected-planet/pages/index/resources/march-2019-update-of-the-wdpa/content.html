The total number of protected area records in the March 2019 release of the World Database on Protected Areas (WDPA) is 242,798 comprising of 222,025 polygons and 20,773 points, covering 245 countries and territories.

Follow us on Facebook @ Protected Planet and Twitter: @protectedplanet

Highlights:

Canada: Partial update, resulting in updated data for all national level protected area records in Québec. This includes the addition of 539 new polygon records made up of 40 protected areas located in the marine realm and 499 terrestrial protected areas. The largest new terrestrial addition is 'Ulittaniujalik' national park, which was designated in 2016 and covers 5,293 km2. The largest marine addition is the 'Aire de concentration d'oiseaux aquatiques deSaint-Hélier 18-10-02', adding a reported 9.74 km2 to the network of water fowl gathering areas along the Peninsule de la Gaspesie.

Jordan – Partial update resulting in the addition of one new polygon record for the 'Dahek Nature Reserve', and further updates to three records for Nature Reserves: the 'Burqu Nature Reserve', which was designated in 2018, and two reserves whose boundaries have been expanded, the 'Azraq Wetland Reserve' and the 'Mujib Nature Reserve'. The Azraq Wetland Reserve, overlaps with a Ramsar Wetland of International Importance.

Peru – Complete update of Peru's national level protected areas resulting in the addition of 10 new polygon records including Private Conservation Areas, Regional Conservation Areas and Communal Reserves.
