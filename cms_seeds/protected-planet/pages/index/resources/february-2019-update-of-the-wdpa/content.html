The total number of protected area records in the February 2019 release of the World Database on Protected Areas (WDPA) is 242,706 comprising of 221,503 polygons and 20,773 points, covering 245 countries and territories.

Follow us on Facebook @ Protected Planet and Twitter: @protectedplanet

Highlights:

Cameroon – Partial update resulting in the addition of new polygon boundaries for theDouala Edéa protected area, which was expanded and upgraded from a Faunal Reserve (IUCN Management Category IV) to a National Park (IUCN Management Category II). The polygon boundaries for the Faunal Reserve were removed.


Ukraine – Partial update resulting in updated polygon boundaries for the Ukrainian section of the natural World Heritage site Primeval Beech Forests of the Carpathians and other regions of Europe. This update reflects an extension made to the site's boundaries in Ukraine, which occurred in 2017. The extension on the site resulted in an increase of its boundaries from 583.53 km2 to 920.23 km2.

WDPA Manual

WDPA User Manual: We are pleased to announce that the WDPA User Manual has been translated into French, Spanish, Russian and Arabic. Additionally, the Manual has been updated with an appendix on WDPA Data Contributor Agreements (Appendix 2), and has undergone general updates and edits based on user feedback. Version 1.5 of the User Manual is now available in five languages via the following links:
