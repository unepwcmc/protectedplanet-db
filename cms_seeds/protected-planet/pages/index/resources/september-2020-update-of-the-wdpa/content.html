The total number of protected area records in the September 2020 release of the World Database on Protected Areas (WDPA) is 262,804 comprising 240,713 polygons and 22,091 points, covering 245 countries and territories.

September 2020 also sees another release of the World Database on other effective area-based conservation measures (WD-OECM). The WD-OECM can be combined with the WDPA to reveal how measures beyond protected areas contribute to conservation networks. It can be downloaded here.

Follow us on Facebook @Protected Planet and Twitter: @protectedplanet

Highlights:

Gambia: This is a complete updates of the national sites of The Gambia. It includes the addition of 13 National protected areas including 12 Community Protected Areas that were designated in 2014 and are managed by the local communities.

Seychelles: Complete update of the Seychelles protected area network, including the expansion of Aldabra Group (Marine) National Park (now 201, 235 km2) and Amirantes (Marine) to Fortune Bank (Marine) Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty (now 217, 588 km2). The 11 new marine protected areas consist of 7 Areas of Outstanding Natural Beauty, and 4 Marine National Parks. This expansion and addition will put national marine protected area coverage at 30%.

ICCAs added to the WDPA: ICCA is an abbreviation for territories and areas conserved by indigenous peoples and local communities. This month sees the addition of one new site in Ecuador and three new sites in Benin. The data for these sites are not for commercial use.
