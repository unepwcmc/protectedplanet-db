Highlights:

Cuba: Complete update of all national-level protected areas. 81 of these protected areas do not have a management plan or designated management authority, so their status has been designated as 'Proposed' following advice from the Cuban data provider, and pending acquisition of further information.

Change in WDPA_PID field

As notified in the previous release, please note that the format of the Parcel ID (WDPA_PID field) has changed from Double to Text. The WDPA_PID field now consists of the WDPAID followed by an underscore (_) and an uppercase letter (for example, the Peru protected area, Pacaya Samiria, which has a WDPAID of 249 has two parcels associated with it and these WDPA_PID's are now listed as 249_A and 249_B). Parcel ID's are assigned to zones of protected areas where data providers have noted differences in terms of management. The Parcel ID is unique to each feature in the WDPA (i.e. each row in the attribute table), while the WDPAID is unique to each protected area in the WDPA. This month's changes affect 616 protected areas, mainly in Australia, Canada, Mexico and Peru.
