The total number of protected area records in the January 2020 release of the World Database on Protected Areas (WDPA) is 248,305 comprising 227,055 polygons and 21,250 points, covering 245 countries and territories.

January 2020 also sees the second release of the World Database on other effective area-based conservation measures (WD-OECM). The WD-OECM can be combined with the WDPA to reveal how measures beyond protected areas contribute to conservation networks. It can be downloaded here.

Follow us on Facebook @Protected Planet and Twitter: @protectedplanet

Highlights:

Australia: a complete update of the country’s protected areas.

Cocos (Keeling) Islands: a complete update of Australia's overseas territories.

Christmas Island: a complete update of Australia's overseas territories.

Heard Island and McDonald Islands: a complete update of Australia's overseas territories.

Norfolk Island: a complete update of Australia's overseas territories.

Uruguay: a complete update of Uruguay’s protected area network. Isla de Flores National Park went from Proposed to Designated status.

Brazil: an update of all national site in Brazil (Indigenous Areas and Indigenous Reserves are not included in this update). Nota Bene: please note that there is an issue with the figure reported in the REP_AREA column for these new sites (from WDPAID 555682352 to WDPAID 555682971). This will be rectified in the February release.

Côte d'Ivoire: an update of national sites( Classified Forests and Botanical Reserves are not including in this update).

Canada: update of Canada's national sites (excluding Québec). The update includes the addition of Wildlife Management Areas, Wildland Provincial Parks, and Marine Protected Areas among other designations.

Israel: the first complete update of the country’s protected areas since 2015.
