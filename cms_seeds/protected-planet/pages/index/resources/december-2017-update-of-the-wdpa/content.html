Follow us on facebook @ Protected Planet and twitter: @protectedplanet Highlights: Areas Beyond National Jurisdiction/Antarctica– Update of the Ross Sea Region Marine Protected Area. The site with an area of over 2 million km2 (the site includes the area under the Ross Ice Shelf, an area of over 1/2 million km2) proposed by the Commission for the Convention on Conservation of Antarctic Marine Living Resources (CCAMLR) was formally designated on the 1st of December 2017 becoming the is the world's largest protected area. Republic of Korea: Complete update to all national records, including the addition of 246 new protected area records covering over 180 km2. The WDPA now includes a total of 1,772 records for South Korea, all of which are in polygon format. Lesotho: Update of the tabular information for the protected areas in Lesotho. Ramsar Sites, Wetlands of International Importance: Addition of new Ramsar sites for Brazil, Italy, Burkina Faso and Democratic Republic of Congo as well as updates to the Ramsar data for Luxembourg and Switzerland.The Democratic Republic of Congo has designated Bassin de la Lufira (the Lufira river basin) as its fourth Wetland of International Importance, this site covers an area of over 43,000 km2. This site covers several existing protected areas as well an additional 18,000 km of land previously unprotected. This update is part of a series of updates on Ramsar data within the WDPA, resulting from enhanced data flows between UNEP-WCMC, the Ramsar Secretariat and member states. Tajikistan: Update of the existing protected areas data set for Tajikistan. Spain: Partial update involving the addition of a record for a second site Comunidade de Montes Veciñais en Man Común de Santiago de Covelo, established through non-legal means by a local community. As noted in the November 2017 WDPA release, records of this type can be identified through their 'Established' status in the STATUS attribute. Croatia: Update of UNESCO Man and Biosphere Reserves for Croatia. A boundary was added for Velebit Mountain UNESCO-MAB Biosphere Reserve replacing the previous point data and the core area of the Mura-Drava-Danube UNESCO-MAB Biosphere Reserve was added. WDPA Manual WDPA User Manual: We are pleased to announce that the WDPA User Manual has been translated into French, Spanish and Russian. Additionally, the Manual has been updated with an appendix on WDPA Data Contributor Agreements (Appendix 2), and has undergone general updates and edits based on user feedback.
