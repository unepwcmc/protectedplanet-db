The total number of protected area records in the July 2016 release of the WDPA is 230,629 comprising of 211,724 polygons and 18,905 points.

Highlights:

Jordan: Update of two protected areas designated under international conventions and agreements in Jordan. The Azraq Oasis Ramsar Site boundary has been updated due to the site expanding, and the Mujib UNESCO-MAB Biosphere Reserve has been converted from a point feature to a polygon following the assignment of a boundary.

Somalia: Removal of all 24 point features and one polygon feature in Somalia. Somalian protected areas were added to the WDPA between 1983 and 1990, prior to the civil war. Due to political instability in the country, the validity of Somalian protected area data included in the WDPA could not be verified since then. In 2014, communications with the governmental data provider could be resumed. Following a 2-year data validation process, the data provider advised removal of all polygon and point features stored from the WPDA. As of July 2016, the country does not have formally established protected areas. However, the creation of one protected area by 2020 is planned as part of Somalia's commitments to the CBD Secretariat and the CBD Aichi Biodiversity Targets (specifically Aichi Target 11). In the absence of protected area data, we would like to draw your attention to the Key Biodiversity Areas data for Somalia available through the IBAT.
