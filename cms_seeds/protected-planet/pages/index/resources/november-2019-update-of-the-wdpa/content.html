The total number of protected area records in the November 2019 release of the World Database on Protected Areas (WDPA) is 245,866 comprising 225,198 polygons and 20,668 points, covering 245 countries and territories.

Follow us on Facebook @ Protected Planet and Twitter: @protectedplanet

Highlights:

Myanmar: A complete update of all national sites in Myanmar, including the addition of Se Taung Wildlife Sanctuary, Hpabaubg Taung Managed Nature Reserve and Htaungwi Taung Geo-features Significant Area. 11 sites have been removed temporarily until their status has been verified, but it is expected that these sites will be submitted again to the WDPA with updated status and boundaries once the gazettement process is complete.

Niue: A complete update of Niue's protected area network. This updated was facilitated by the Secretariat for the Pacific Regional Environment Programme, supported by the BIOPAMA programme.

Timor-Leste: A complete update of Timor-Leste's protected area network, made possible by a successful in-country workshop supported by the BIOPAMA programme, and organised by Conservation International and IUCN.

Tuvalu: A complete update of Tuvalu's protected area network. Five protected areas have been divided into zones, including components that operate as Locally Managed Marine Areas (LMMAs). The fact that all of Tuvalu's protected areas are under the governance of local communities is now reflected in the WDPA. This update was facilitated by the Secretariat for the Pacific Regional Environment Programme, supported by the BIOPAMA programme. It has been made possible by the Tuvalu Ridge to Reef project.
