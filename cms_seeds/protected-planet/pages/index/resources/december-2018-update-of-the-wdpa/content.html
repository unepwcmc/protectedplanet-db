The total number of protected area records in the October 2018 release of the World Database on Protected Areas (WDPA) is 235,585 comprising of 218,669 polygons and 16,916 points, covering 245 countries and territories.

Follow us on Facebook @ Protected Planet and twitter: @protectedplanet

Highlights:

Canada: Partial update: Update of all nationally designated protected areas (except in Quebec). This included the the verification of approximately 4,000 sites and the addition of 160 new polygon records, with the largest of these sites being Tallurutiup (Lancaster Sound) National Marine Conservation Area which has a total area of 109,000 km2. There are also a number of indigenous led protected areas such as Van Tat K'atr'anahtii (Old Crow Flats) First Nation Settlement Land.

Tanzania: Partial update: Inclusion of a new protected area (Minziro Nature Forest Reserve). This protected area was first established a Forest Reserve in 1947 and was redesignated as a Nature Forest Reserve in 2016 due to its high biological diversity values.

Protected Planet Report 2018:UNEP-WCMC and partners recently launched the Protected Planet Report 2018 at the CBD COP in Egypt, describing the current status of the world's global protected area coverage. Just under 15% of the world's terrestrial and inland waters, over 16% of the coastal and marine areas within national jurisdiction, and approximately 7.3% of the global ocean are covered by protected areas. Please click here to access the report and here to access the website report.

2018 United Nations List of Protected Areas - Supplement on protected area management effectiveness: UNEP-WCMC and partners also launched a new edition of the United Nations List of Protected Areas focused on protected area management effectiveness. While the quantitative aspect of Aichi Target 11 is on track to be reached, further efforts are required to achieve the quantitative elements of the Target, including on the effectiveness of management. Indeed, information on management effectiveness is available for just under 1% of the global protected area estate. The report, along with the countries' official list of protected areas, is available in English and in French here.
