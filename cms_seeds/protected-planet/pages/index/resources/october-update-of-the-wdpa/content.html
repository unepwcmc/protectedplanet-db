The total number of protected area records in the September 2016 release of the WDPA is 232,271 comprising of 213,442 polygons and 18,829 points.

Highlights:

Natura 2000: We have received an update of the EU 28 Natura 2000 network. The new update consists of 29,271 records- which is an update of 81 from the previous version, there have been a number of sites added (499 in all) and removed (418 in all) from several countries . There has been a net addition of 41,763 km² to the network principally in the form of new marine sites from Portugal and Spain. See attached excel table for more detailed information. This update is based upon the “end 2015" version of the Natura 2000 database- this is the composite version of all updates by EU Member States made throughout 2015.

Malta: In addition to the Natura 2000 up date, Malta supplied us with 11 new records which were created in May 2016. The new records are all marine sites and add an additional 3,450 km² of protected areas to Maltese waters.

OSPAR: Marine Protected Areas – Full update of Marine Protected Areas in the OSPAR network in the North-East Atlantic, including the addition of 411 protected areas.

Republic of Korea: Complete update of protected areas, including the addition of 951 new polygon records.

Côte d'Ivoire:Partial update of 17 sites.

Montserrat: Full update of protected areas data for Montserrat which has been significantly affected by the eruptions of the Soufrière Hills volcano. The continued eruptions of the Soufrière Hills volcano since the early 1990's of this volcano has resulted in the destruction of a large portion of the island including the majority of existing protected areas. The only remaining protected area on the island is the Centre Hills Protected Forest Area.

Saint Helena, Ascension and Tristan da Cunha: Complete date of data for the island of St Helena including the newly designated St. Helena MPA which has an area of 444,916km². Due to a request from the St Helena government all 16 protected areas for this islands are restricted from public dissemination, they are however allowed to be included in global analysis performed by UNEP-WCMC. For access to this data please contact Environment and Planning Development Section, Development & Economic Planning Department, St Helena.

World Heritage sites: Complete update of all 229 natural and mixed World Heritage sites. Following the 40th Session of the World Heritage Committee in Istanbul (Turkey), six new natural sites were inscribed: Archipiélago de Revillagigedo, Mexico; Hubei Shennongjia, China; Lut Desert, Iran; Mistaken Point, Canada; Sanganeb Marine National Park and Dungonab Bay – Mukkawar Island Marine National Park, Sudan; and Western Tien-Shan, Kazakhstan, Kyrgyzstan, Uzbekistan. In addition, three new mixed sites were inscribed: Ennedi Massif: Natural and Cultural Landscape, Chad; Khangchendzonga National Park, India; and The Ahwar of Southern Iraq: Refuge of Biodiversity and the Relict Landscape of the Mesopotamian Cities; Iraq.

Protected Planet Report 2016: UNEP-WCMC and partners recently launched the Protected Planet Report 2016, describing the current status of the world's global protected area coverage. Just under 15% of the world's terrestrial and inland waters, over 10% of the coastal and marine areas within national jurisdiction, and approximately 4% of the global ocean are covered by protected areas. Please click the following link to access the report: http://wcmc.io/protectedplanetreport_2016.

WDPA User Manual:We are pleased to announce that the WDPA User Manual has been translated into French and Spanish. Additionally, the Manual has been updated with an appendix on WDPA Data Contributor Agreements (Appendix 2), and has undergone general updates and edits based on user feedback. Change in WDPA_PID field

Please note, there will be a slight change to the structure of the Parcel ID's (WDPA_PID) field from November's release. Parcel ID's are assigned to zones of protected areas where data providers have noted differences in terms of management. The PID is unique to each feature in the WDPA (i.e. each line in the attribute table), while the WDPAID is unique to each protected area in the WDPA. The format of this field will change to allow relationships between the WDPA_PID and WDPAID fields to be more easily recognised. The WDPA_PID field type will change from Double to Text, and will consist of the WDPAID followed by an underscore (_) and an uppercase letter. For example, the Peru protected area, Pacaya Samiria, which has a WDPAID of 249 has two parcels associated with it and these WDPA_PID's will be listed as 249_A and 249_B in the November release. This change in structure is needed for 611 protected areas in the WDPA. If the structure of these fields is important in your workflow or automated models, you will need to adjust these accordingly.
