The total number of protected area records in the December 2016 release of the WDPA is 232,128 comprising of 213,328 polygons and 18,800 points.

Follow us on Facebook @Protected Planet and Twitter: @protectedplanet

Highlights:

Baltic Sea Protected Areas (HELCOM): The network of protected areas designated under the Helsinki Convention is fully represented in the WDPA. Collaboration with the HELCOM Secretariat resulted in the addition of 21 protected areas and update of 148 records.

Peru: Complete update of national-level protected areas, including the addition of 45 protected areas. 58 protected areas designated as 'Zona de Amortiguamiento' were removed following advice from the Peruvian data provider. These records were buffer zones for existing protected areas, which do not meet the IUCN definition of a protected area due to less stringent management approaches.

Ramsar sites: Complete update of Ramsar sites. Records within the WDPA are now aligned with the official data made available via the Ramsar Sites Information Service, however the percentage of records with boundary information is higher within the WDPA. Contracting parties to the Ramsar Convention primarily submit point data to the Secretariat. To increase data accuracy, UNEP-WCMC approaches national data providers to acquire additional boundary information. As a result 60% of Ramsar sites within the WDPA are in polygon format, compared to only 30% within the original Ramsar dataset. A long term collaboration with the Secretariat is under discussion to ensure that the boundary information is identical in both databases.

Tanzania: Update of all national-level terrestrial protected areas, resulting in the addition of 210 protected area records.

United States of America – Partial update to the Papahānaumokuākea Marine National Monument, the largest marine protected area in the world, following expansion of the protected area in August 2016.This site now has an area of just over 1.5 million km².

Palau: Addition of the of the recently designated Palau National Marine Sanctuary with an area of 500,000 km², which will preserve 80% of its EEZ as a no-take area protected from all exploitation. The Marine Sanctuary will be fully functioning by 2020.

Pitcairn Islands: Addition of the Pitcairn Islands Marine Reserve which has an area of over 860,000 km².This site was formally designated in September 2016.

Chile: Updated data set for Chile including the addition of the Nazca-Desventuradas Marine Park which has an area of over 3000,000 km².

WDPA Manual

WDPA User Manual:We are pleased to announce that the WDPA User Manual has been translated into French and Spanish. Additionally, the Manual has been updated with an appendix on WDPA Data Contributor Agreements (Appendix 2), and has undergone general updates and edits based on user feedback.
