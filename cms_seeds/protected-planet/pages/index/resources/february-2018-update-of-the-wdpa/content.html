The total number of protected area records in the February 2018 release of the WDPA is 236,231 comprising of 217,825 polygons and 18,406 point, covering 245 countries and territories.

Follow us on facebook @ Protected Planet and twitter: @protectedplanet

Highlights:

South Africa: Complete update of all national-level protected area records. This update has resulted in an increase in the quality of data for South Africa, including the reclassification of eight biosphere reserves as UNESCO Man and Biosphere Reserves, which were previously listed as nationally designated protected areas.

Togo: Update of protected areas attribute information for several site.

Myanmar: Complete update of all national-level protected areas. This is the first comprehensive update of Myanmar's protected area records since 2011, resulting in 12 new polygon and four new point records being added to the country's protected area network. The current information stored in the WDPA for Myanmar was also reviewed and updated to ensure an accurate representation of the country's protected areas.

Malawi: Partial update to protected area records for Malawi, resulting in updated boundaries for nine polygon records and updated data for one point record. This is the first update of National Parks and Wildlife Reserves since 2002.

Swaziland: Complete update involving the addition of one polygon record and updated boundaries for 20 polygon records. The new polygon record is for Lomati Nature Reserve (WDPA_ID = 555626094), which represents an area of 6.63 km2.

Finland: Added one new site, Linnunsuo which is a Private Nature Reserve , which is an example of a protected area conserved by a local community in Finland. The site is owned by a local NGO, but managed by the local community in collaboration with a government agency.

Ramsar Sites, Wetlands of International Importance: Addition of new Ramsar sites for France and Poland with updated Rasmar sites for Argentina, Myanmar and Swaziland. This update is part of a series of updates on Ramsar data within the WDPA, resulting from enhanced data flows between UNEP-WCMC, the Ramsar Secretariat and member states.

Spain: Update of IUCN Management category and Management Agency for nearly 4,000 sites.

We updated the "Sub Location" (SUB_LOC) field to standardised the field to use just semicolons (removing commas and spaces). We also corrected several errors in the assigning of the "Parent Country" (Parent_ISO3) for example several sites in Greenland were incorrectly attributed.

WDPA Manual

WDPA User Manual: We are pleased to announce that the WDPA User Manual has been translated into French, Spanish and Russian. Additionally, the Manual has been updated with an appendix on WDPA Data Contributor Agreements (Appendix 2), and has undergone general updates and edits based on user feedback. Version 1.5 of the User Manual is now available in three languages through the geodatabase download above and via the following links:
