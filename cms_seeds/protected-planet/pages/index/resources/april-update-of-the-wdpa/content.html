The total number of protected area records in the April 2016 release of the WDPA is 220,877 comprising of 201,933 polygons and 18,944 points.

Highlights:

Brazil: Update of national-level protected areas, except for areas under indigenous governance.

Cayman Islands: Complete update of national-level and protected areas designated under international conventions and agreements.

Mexico: Complete update of all national-level and protected areas designated under international conventions and agreements. There are now only point records for Ramsar sites, which the National Commission of Natural Protected Areas (CONANP) is aiming to update in the coming months. CONANP has further requested the removal of sites designated at the provincial and state level, which are undergoing internal validation.

New Zealand: Update of national-level protected areas, including the reclassification of all protected areas as either Marine or Terrestrial.

Saint Helena: All 16 records on the island of Saint Helena have been removed from the WDPA following a request from the local authority, this includes a proposed Marine Protected Area which covers the entire EEZ of St Helena. There are 11 records remaining under the 'SHN' ISO code, which cover the islands of Ascension Island and Tristan Da Cunha. For more information on those sites removed please visit the Saint Helena government website.

Specially Protected Areas of Mediterranean Importance (Barcelona Convention; SPAMI): Complete update of protected areas designated under this regional designation. Polygon data is now available for all of records.
