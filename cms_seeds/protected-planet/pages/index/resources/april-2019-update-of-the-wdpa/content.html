The total number of protected area records in the April 2019 release of the World Database on Protected Areas (WDPA) is 242,783 comprising 222,015 polygons and 20, 768 points, covering 245 countries and territories.

Follow us on Facebook @ Protected Planet and Twitter: @protectedplanet

Highlights:

Eswatini: This is a complete update of Eswatini's protected area network. Notable change is the addition of Lubuyane Nature Reserve to the WDPA. 11 sites that do not meet the definition of a protected area have been removed, but will be included in our Other Effective Area-based Conservation Measures (OECM) database later in 2019. These Conserved Areas are contributing to conservation but do not have conservation as the primary objective.

Solomon Islands: An update of the descriptive information for 51 of the protected areas in the Solomon Islands. Five point records have been deleted as they were identified as duplicates of polygon records. This is the first ever government-approved update of the country's data. It was facilitated by the Secretariat for the Pacific Regional Environment Programme, supported by the BIOPAMA programme.
